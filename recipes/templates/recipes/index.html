Introduction

Bendakai is Kriti Godey's honours project for her computer science degree. Its goal to be able to answer questions like "I've got six potatoes, an onion and milk. What healthy food can I cook with it?" i.e. it aims to provide you with recipes you like based on what you've liked before, nutritional information, and price of ingredients. It will also be a conventional recipe website; you can search for recipes and keep track of your recipe collection.

That sounds cool! What can I do?

Well, I need recipes to recommend! I already have a bunch of recipes, but they aren't separated in a way that makes it easy to compute nutritional values and other data from them. You can help me by correcting a recipe!. There's a recipe and a form, copying one recipe over should take a couple of minutes. This will really help me. You can also add a recipe! and keep track of your recipe collection here! You'll need to log in, but you can use your Facebook, Twitter or OpenID account to do so, so it's relatively painless.

You can also rate the recipes that are here, and rate ingredients. This just involves clicking a button and is very valuable in finding out what user prefer so I can improve my recipe recommendation.

Bug reports, complaints, suggestions, tips, advice, feedback, comments, questions etc. are much appreciated. E-mail them to me at kriti.godey@gmail.com.

What's with the name?

The name Bendakai means "okra" or "ladyfinger" in Telugu, my native language. It's food related, fairly easy to pronounce, and most importantly, the top level domain was available.

Progress and Plans

I'm currently working with two different recommendation approaches and randomly generated data. I hope to be able to test with real data soon, which is why I need recipes. I'm trying to update this website as fast as possible.
